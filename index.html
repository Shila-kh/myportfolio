I am a PhD candidate at the Faculty of Business and Law, Curtin University, exploring the future of sustainable agri-food supply chains. My research focuses on how artificial intelligence can become a strategic capability strengthening resilience, competitiveness, and environmental performance. By bridging technology, sustainability, and strategic management, I examine how organisations can move beyond efficiency-driven operations to systems that withstand climate risks, resource constraints, and shifting consumer expectations. Working closely with industry stakeholders, I investigate how AI can transform the way supply chains sense risks, make decisions, and reconfigure operations for long-term sustainability..

My Research

My research combines qualitative insights and advanced statistical modelling to uncover how AI investments translate into real sustainability outcomes such as reduced waste, improved traceability, stronger resilience, and better operational and financial performance.
Through an sequential mixed-methods design, I aim to develop a capability-based framework that helps industry leaders understand where AI creates value, what conditions enable successful adoption, and how organisations can strategically harness AI to meet sustainability, regulatory, and market expectations. Ultimately, my work provides evidence-based guidance for policymakers, producers, processors, logistics providers, and retailers who are seeking practical, scalable pathways to accelerate sustainable transformation in the agri-food sector.

Project
Transportation Management System (TMS) Implementation (SAP-based)

For a leading international transport company managing complex, large-scale freight operations, we successfully spearheaded the transformation from fragmented legacy systems to a fully integrated SAP Transportation Management System. Over a compressed six-month implementation, we centralized end-to-end logistics processes from contract negotiation and order execution to dispatch, tracking, and financial settlement streamlining operations across a geographically dispersed network. By harmonizing master data, designing 36 critical system customizations, and implementing robust process controls, the project delivered unprecedented operational visibility, financial clarity, and cross-departmental synchronization. Strategic phased Go-Live execution minimized risk, while post-launch innovations, including AI-driven automation for high-volume transactions, continue to optimize efficiency and resource utilization. The result: a future-ready, fully unified transport management ecosystem driving performance, compliance, and scalable growth.
